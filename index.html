Hello World!

My name is Nick Linck and I am currently a Junior at the University of Michigan pursuing a bachelors degree in Computer Science Engineering and a minor in physics. I am looking for opportunities to expand my computer science knowledge and to develop professionally as a software engineer. My coursework at the University of Michigan has focused primarily on programming with C++ and some python. Using these languages, I have completed countless programming projects focused on learning new data structures and algorithms and making those programs run with the smallest time and space complexity. Outside of the classroom I have worked on personal projects and competed in hackathons where I have used many other languages such as HTML, CSS, Java Script, JQuery, Java, Arduino, R, and Ruby. In my current position, I work as a software development engineering intern for Thomson Reuters. I am on the 1040 US tax team where I work with tax analysts to develop and maintain Ultra Tax CS (the companies tax software) and also add new features to internal tools used by the analysts and the programmers. All of this work is done in either C++ or C#.
